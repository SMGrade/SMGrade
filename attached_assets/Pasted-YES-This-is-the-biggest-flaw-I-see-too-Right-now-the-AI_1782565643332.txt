YES!! This is the biggest flaw I see too.

Right now the AI is thinking:

"Highest damage shield = best recommendation."

That's wrong for a progression game.

It should think like an experienced SwordMasters player.

I'd change the recommendation logic to something like this:

Improve the recommendation engine.

Never recommend an item simply because it has the highest damage or multiplier.

Recommendations must consider progression.

Before suggesting any sword or shield, evaluate:

Can the player realistically afford it?
Is it appropriate for their current level?
Is it appropriate for their current power?
Is it the next logical upgrade instead of an endgame item?
Will it provide good value for its cost?

Do not recommend equipment that is far beyond the player's progression.

Instead, recommend the next realistic upgrade path.

For example:

Incorrect:
"Buy Asgardian Aegis."

Correct:
"Your next upgrade should be Celestial Bulwark. It is affordable for your current progression and provides a significant damage increase. Asgardian Aegis should be your long-term goal after reaching approximately X power and earning more gold."

For swords, understand that swords generally contribute less to total damage than damage multipliers from shields.

Prioritize recommendations in this order unless the player's data indicates otherwise:

Damage multiplier improvements.
Shield upgrades.
Power progression.
Sword upgrades.
Level progression.

Every recommendation must explain WHY it was chosen.

Never recommend unrealistic or impossible upgrades.

The website should behave like an experienced SwordMasters veteran giving practical advice rather than simply selecting the strongest item in the database.
